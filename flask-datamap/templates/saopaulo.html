"After the drought" in Sao Paulo




A mega city




Sao Paulo is the most populous city in Brazil, and one of the most populous city of the Americas (behind New York and Mexico). It ranks as the 6th most populous city on Earth with 21 millions habitants (with suburb).








































Major drought in 2014



In 2015, Sao Paulo faced its greatest water crisis in over 80 years. Millions of its residents have faced daily water shutoffs. Climate change was one of the main cause of the problem, decreasing rainfall in this area. Poor water infrastructure have made the situation worse.



Main reservoir system of Cantareira supplying São Paulo being at only 6% of its capacity in early February 2015, the water utility of Sao Paulo has reduced water pressure in the system.


















Floods in 2015



Heavy rains during 2015 brought flooding to the street of Sao Paulo. Water doesn't always where we want it to go : despite torrential rainfall, some area of the region was still suffering from drought







In late 2015, reservoir system was only filled at 50%.






































2016 conclusions



2015-2016 water crisis was a chock for Sao Paulo habitant. Two lessons came outside of the drought :



Maintain water infrastructures :
More than 30 percent of São Paulo’s drinking water is estimated to be lost to leaks in the distribution network

Protect existing vegetation :
forest provide a cheap way to clean and keep water. Deforestation was one of the main cause of this drought/flood crisis.

Invest in wastewater recycling :
such measures can help meet water demands without the need for costly long-distance water transfers.
